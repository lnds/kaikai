## What region{} does, in one paragraph

`region { ... }` is an expression. Inside the block, list/record/variant
constructors are routed to a **bump-arena** (`kai_arena_cons`, sentinel
`rc=INT32_MAX` so `decref` is a no-op) instead of the reference-counted
heap. At the closing brace the arena frees in one shot. The block's
value is wrapped in `kai_deep_copy_out(...)`: a scalar passes through
free, but any pointer that **escapes** the brace is deep-copied out of
the arena onto the RC heap with `rc=1` before the arena dies. That
deep-copy is the entire cost story.

## Method (a previous region bench failed by measuring 0.06 s = noise)

1. Every case is **two byte-identical `.kai` programs** â€” one with
   `region {`, one with a bare `{` â€” same logic, same result. They live
   in `examples/perceus/region_bench_*.kai`.
2. For each region program, the emitted C is grepped for
   `kai_arena_cons`. **If it is absent, the routing did not happen and
   the result is a method failure, not a measurement.** (This caught the
   escape-aux case below â€” constructors in a *called helper* are not
   arena-routed.)
3. Primary metric = **allocations** via `KAI_TRACE_RC` (`-DKAI_TRACE_RC=1`,
   run with `KAI_TRACE_RC=1 KAI_TRACE_RC_QUIET=1`): heap `alloc_total`,
   `incref_total`, and `arena_alloc`/`arena_free`. Secondary = **wall**,
   best-of-5, with N driven up to 4 000 000 so the time is well clear of
   sub-second noise.
4. C and LLVM backends are checked for identical output on every region
   program (`KAI_BACKEND=llvm`); all three pass.

## Findings

**1. In the escape-free niche, region is a clear win â€” and the win is
real, not cosmetic.** It erases **94 % of heap allocations**
(6 399 724 â†’ 399 724), **100 % of increfs** (5 400 000 â†’ 0), and runs
**1.72Ã— faster**. Every cons cell skips the RC machinery and the arena
frees in one bulk `arena_pop`. This is the LIFO-scratch case the feature
was designed for, and it delivers.

   The headline `arena_alloc=6 000 000` with `incref_total=0` is the
   whole story: six million cells, allocated and freed, that never
   touched a refcount.

**2. On escape, region is pure overhead â€” measured, not asserted.** When
the structure crosses the brace, `deep_copy_out` rebuilds it on the RC
heap. The inline case is the sharpest proof: the heap RC counts are
**byte-identical** between the two variants (`alloc_total=2 386 898`,
`cons=2 000 001`, `incref=1 800 000` in both). The region variant just
adds **2 000 000 wasted arena allocs** on top â€” the cells it built in the
arena and then threw away by copying out. Result: **1.63Ã— slower**.

**3. The aux case is worse, and it is the one that matters for the
original motivation.** Constructors inside a *helper* called from the
block are **not arena-routed** (grep confirms zero `kai_arena_cons`), so
`deep_copy_out` is a pure heapâ†’heap re-copy of the whole 50-cell list
every iteration. The region variant does **~10 M extra heap allocs**
(20 397 384 vs 10 397 384) and **leaks 50Ã— more** (10 198 692 vs 198 692
â€” the deep-copied lists are not reclaimed in this shape). It runs
**1.30Ã— slower**. This is the case that models a compiler: a lexer â†’
parser â†’ typer pipeline builds its AST in helper functions and returns
it, so **almost everything escapes phase-to-phase**, and almost nothing
is arena-routed. The original "wrap a compiler phase in a region" idea
lands squarely in this column.

**4. region only routes constructors that are *lexically inside* the
block.** This is the structural ceiling. A `region { build(50, []) }`
arena-allocates nothing, because `build`'s cons cells are emitted with
`in_region = false`. To benefit, the throwaway allocation must be written
inline in the block â€” which is exactly the scratch-fold shape, and
exactly *not* the shape of code that calls helpers.

## Verdict

`region{}` **meets a real but narrow acceptance bar.** The mechanics are
sound, C/LLVM agree, and in the escape-free LIFO-scratch niche the
benefit is large and reproducible (âˆ’94 % heap allocs, 1.72Ã— wall). That
niche is genuine: a hand-written hot loop that builds throwaway
aggregates and folds them to a scalar, with the allocation inline.

But the benefit **does not generalize to the case #120 was originally
motivated by.** The moment a structure escapes â€” which is the norm for a
compiler pipeline, and the norm for "build a thing and return it" â€” the
v1 deep-copy-out policy makes region a net loss (1.3â€“1.6Ã— slower, more
allocs, more leak). region has no way to capture allocation done in
called helpers, which is where a real phase does its building.

**Recommendation: close #120 as *mechanics delivered, niche identified,
general benefit not demonstrated*.** Specifically:

- Keep the feature. It is correct and it wins where it wins.
- Document it honestly as a **hand-applied, escape-free scratch optimization**,
  not as a compiler-wide allocation strategy. The `region_scratch.kai`
  framing is right; the "use it in the compiler" framing is refuted by
  case 3.
- The path to making region pay on escaping data is the deferred
  **TyBranded / zero-copy-escape** follow-up (brand-in-signature, no
  deep-copy-out) noted in `region_return_value.kai`. Until that lands,
  region's addressable market is escape-free folds only. Do **not** wire
  region into compiler phases on the strength of this feature as shipped.
